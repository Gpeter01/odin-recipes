Vegetable Soup

Description

Nigerian vegetable soup is more than just a meal, it’s a celebration simmering in a pot. Bursting with color, aroma, and soul, it brings together the earthy freshness of green leaves like ugu (fluted pumpkin), waterleaf, or bitterleaf, dancing in harmony with tender chunks of meat, stockfish, and dried fish. The palm oil glows like sunset gold, coating every ingredient in a rich, velvety sheen that speaks of warmth and home. Each stir releases a melody of flavors — the umami depth of crayfish, the spice of pepper, and the comforting thickness of well-seasoned broth, a taste that hugs the heart as much as it fills the belly.

But beyond its taste, vegetable soup carries stories of mothers bent over steaming pots, of laughter around family tables, of generations passing down recipes with love and pride. It’s a dish that unites, whether served with pounded yam in the east, eba in the west, or semovita in the north. Every bite is a journey through Nigeria’s cultural soul — bold, resilient, and endlessly nourishing. To eat it is to taste the rhythm of the land, the fire of its people, and the tenderness of tradition itself.

Ingredients

Here are the ingredients to make the vegetable soup at home:

  • Leafy greens: Common choices are spinach, ugu (fluted pumpkin leaves), waterleaf, efo shoko (Lagos spinach), or afang leaves.
  • Palm oil
  • Tomato paste
  • Onions
  • Assorted meat/protein
  • Pepper
  • Salt
  • Curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, salt to taste, knorr, ginger and garlic etc.

    • Optional, but I also recommend crayfish, seasoning cubes (Knorr) and locut beans

    Steps

    1. Wash and chop vegetables, rinse stockfish and dried fish with hot water to soften.
    2. Season the assorted meats with onions, salt and seasoning cubes, add a little water and cook until tender.
    3. Add the stockfish and dried fish to the cooked meat and simmer together so the flabours blend deeply.
    4. Pour palm oil into the pot and allow it to melt into the broth, stir well until it gives the soup a rich, reddish colour.
    5. Add ground crayfish, pepper and extra seasoning to taste, let it ismmer for 5-10 minutes for the flavours to combine.
    6. Stir in the chopped ugu (and waterleaf if using).
    7. Taste the soup and adjust salt or seasoning if needed, the texture should be thick, rich and full of flavour.
    8. Pait with pounded yam, eba, fufu, or semovita.

    Enjoy your flavourful, hearty vegetable soup!
