Jollof rice

Description

Jollof Rice is more than just a meal, it’s a fiery celebration on a plate, the heartbeat of every Nigerian gathering. Each grain is kissed by rich, red tomato stew simmered in a perfect blend of peppers, onions, and aromatic spices. The smoky undertone, born from a well-loved pot and patient cooking, wraps every bite in nostalgia. It’s vibrant, bold, and unapologetically flavorful — the kind of dish that doesn’t whisper but sings, loud and proud, that this is Nigeria’s soul food. Whether cooked over open firewood or in a modern kitchen, the aroma alone can pull neighbors from down the street.

But Jollof Rice isn’t just food, it’s identity. It’s the dish that unites and divides, the subject of endless debates with Ghana and Senegal, yet always crowned king in the hearts of Nigerians. Served with fried plantains, grilled chicken, or smoky suya, it brings people together, from wedding halls to roadside parties. It’s the taste of laughter, music, and togetherness; a symbol of home that every Nigerian, no matter where they are, carries in their memory and their heart.

Ingredients

Here are the ingredients to make your delicious jollof rice at home:

  • Rice (Long-grain parboiled rice is commonly used)
  • A blend of red bell peppers (tatashe), tomatoes, and scotch bonnet peppers for heat.
  • Tomato paste
  • Onions
  • Your preferred protein
  • Cooking oil
  • Butter
  • Curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, salt to taste, knorr, ginger and garlic etc.

    • Optional, but I also recommend crayfish and nutmeg.

    Steps

    1. Blend fresh tomatoes, red bell peppers, Scotch bonnet (ata rodo), and onions into a smooth paste.
    2. Partially cook the rice for a few minutes, then rinse and set aside
    3. Heat oil, sauté chopped onions, then fry the blended mixture until thick and the oil separates.
    4. Add tomato paste, stock cubes, thyme, curry, bay leaf, salt, and chicken stock. Simmer for flavor.
    5. Stir in the parboiled rice, mix well so it absorbs the sauce evenly.
    6. Cover tightly (using foil if needed) and let the rice cook on low heat, steaming until soft and smoky.
    7. tir gently, adjust seasoning, and serve with fried plantains, grilled chicken, or salad.

    Enjoy!
